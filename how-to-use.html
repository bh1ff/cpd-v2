Getting Started

How to Use This CPD

A practical guide for facilitators delivering the Digital Youth Work CPD programme.

PREPBefore you run a session

What you'll need

  • A room with Wi-Fi, a projector, and enough space for small groups
  • Participants with smartphones or laptops (works on either)
  • Sticky notes, pens, and a whiteboard or flipchart
  • Accounts for Kahoot, Mentimeter, and Padlet (free tiers are fine)
  • 15 minutes to read the facilitator guide before each session
STRUCTUREEvery session follows the same 5-phase pattern
1. Discover15 min

An opening question and warm-up. Surface assumptions before the activity.

2. Activity60 min

The main practical block. Multiple activities, with real tools (Kahoot, Padlet, Mentimeter).

3. Key Ideas20 min

Brief theory input. Keep it to 15–20 minutes — the slides do the heavy lifting.

4. Discussion55 min

Structured group discussion. Participants apply the learning to their own practice.

5. Closing30 min

A closing question and a takeaway action. Every session ends with something participants will actually do.

FACILITATIONTips for a good session

What makes these sessions work

  • Practical first. Everyone plays the tool before we talk about the tool.
  • Discussion-heavy. You're not lecturing. You're designing good conversations.
  • Running over is fine. Skip an activity rather than rush it. Each block has more activities than you need.
  • The room is the learning. Where sticky notes go on the wall matters more than where they go on a slide.
  • Use their language. If participants say "I wouldn't use that", ask why and build from there.
