Session 2 of 6

Your Values Under Pressure

Practitioners test whether their stated values survive contact with real digital design and live pressure.

📊 Download Slides (.pptx) 📄 Facilitator Guide (.docx)

Learning Objectives (LIA Template)

Aim

Practitioners test whether their stated values survive contact with real digital design and live pressure.

Objectives

  • Articulate a personal youth work value in writing
  • Design a digital activity that deliberately embeds that value
  • Observe whether the value is visible to others in the design
  • Reflect on the gap between intention and observable behaviour

Outcomes

  • Know how to: Design a simple digital interaction using Padlet or Mentimeter
  • Understand: There is often a gap between stated values and actual design decisions
  • Equipped with: The habit of asking 'What value am I embedding in this design?'
  • Action: Review one activity you currently run and identify the values it expresses

Session Plan

Structured across five phases (D-A-K-D-C). All activities include tool, timing, and instructions.

DISCOVER0 – 15 min

"What do you actually believe about youth work — and does it hold up when things go wrong?"

Everyone has values they say they hold. This session tests whether those values show up in what you actually build and how you react when things don't go to plan.

Values Brainstorm5 min🛠 Mentimeter

Submit your top 3 youth work values to a word cloud. What comes up most? What's surprising?

Rank Your Top 35 min

From the word cloud, pick YOUR top 3 values. Write them on a card in order. Keep the card — you'll need it later.

ACTIVITY15 – 75 min
Seal Your Value5 min

Pick ONE value from your card. Write it on a slip of paper. Seal it in an envelope. This is the value you're going to deliberately embed in a digital activity. Don't tell anyone what it is.

Build It25 min🛠 Padlet or Mentimeter

In pairs, design a 10-minute digital activity that embeds your sealed value. Use Padlet (collaborative board) or Mentimeter (polls/Q&A). Your activity must: have clear instructions, be something a young person could actually do, and embed your value in a way that should be observable.

Run It + Observe20 min

Swap with another pair. They run YOUR activity without your help. You sit back and watch. Observers: note down where you think their value shows up. What does the design tell you about what they believe?

Envelope Reveal10 min

Open the envelopes. Read the value out loud. Did the observers guess right? Where did the value show up? Where was it missing? Be honest — the gap is where the learning is.

KEY IDEAS75 – 95 min

The Gap Between What We Say and What We Do

  • Stated values vs observable behaviour — they're often different, and that's normal
  • Under pressure (disengagement, tech failure, time running out), your real priorities come out
  • System constraints force trade-offs: you can't have full freedom AND full safety at the same time
  • The question isn't 'what do I believe?' — it's 'what did I actually build?'
Pressure Scenarios10 min

Facilitator reads 4 pressure scenarios. For each, participants write which of their top 3 values they'd sacrifice first.

Examples

  • Half the group isn't engaging. You have 5 minutes left. Do you push on or change the plan?
  • A young person says something offensive in the chat. Do you delete it, address it publicly, or message them privately?
  • The Wi-Fi goes down mid-session. You have 20 young people looking at you. What do you do?
  • Two participants are dominating. Others have gone silent. Do you intervene or let it play out?
DISCUSSION95 – 150 min
Value Trade-Off Cards20 min

Each table gets a set of cards with value pairs that can conflict: Safety vs Freedom, Inclusion vs Efficiency, Fun vs Learning, Control vs Agency. Pick one pair. Design a scenario where you HAVE to choose. Present your dilemma to the group.

Redesign Under Constraint20 min🛠 Padlet

Go back to your activity from earlier. Now add a constraint: half the group doesn't have smartphones / one participant has anxiety about being visible / you only have 5 minutes instead of 10. Redesign on Padlet. What did you have to change? What value did you have to compromise?

Fishbowl Discussion15 min

4 chairs in the middle. Volunteers sit in them and discuss: 'When is it OK to compromise your values in youth work?' Everyone else listens. Anyone can tap in to replace a chair. Facilitator keeps time.

CLOSING150 – 180 min

"If the platform disappeared tomorrow, what principles would still guide you?"

Tools change. Apps shut down. But the way you think about young people's needs doesn't have to. The principle matters more than the platform.

Principle Statement10 min

Write one sentence: 'Regardless of what tool I use, I will always ___.' Share with the group. Pin them all on a board.

Exit Ticket5 min

Sticky note: One thing you learnt. One thing you'll change. One question you have.

Your Takeaway
Review one activity you currently run. What values does it express? What values are missing?
