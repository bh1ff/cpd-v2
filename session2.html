Session 2 of 6

Your Values Under Pressure

Practitioners test whether their stated values survive contact with real digital design and live pressure.

📊 Download Slides (.pptx) 📄 Facilitator Guide (.docx)

Learning Objectives (LIA Template)

Aim

Practitioners test whether their stated values survive contact with real digital design and live pressure.

Objectives

  • Articulate a personal youth work value in writing
  • Design a digital activity that deliberately embeds that value
  • Observe whether the value is visible to others in the design
  • Reflect on the gap between intention and observable behaviour

Outcomes

  • Know how to: Design a simple digital interaction using Padlet or Mentimeter
  • Understand: There is often a gap between stated values and actual design decisions
  • Equipped with: The habit of asking 'What value am I embedding in this design?'
  • Action: Review one activity you currently run and identify the values it expresses

Session Plan

Structured across five phases (D-A-K-D-C). Each phase has more activities than you can fit — pick what matches your group.

DISCOVER 0 – 15 min 5 activities — pick what fits

"What are the hidden and explicit values of the digital youth work curriculum?"

Every activity you run teaches values — some of them you meant to put there, some of them you didn't. This session surfaces both: the values your practice says out loud, and the values it quietly shows through design, default settings, and what you do under pressure.

Values Brainstorm🛠 Mentimeter

Submit your top 3 youth work values to a word cloud. What comes up most? What's surprising? What's missing that should be there?

Rank Your Top 3🛠 Mentimeter

From the word cloud, pick YOUR top 3 values and rank them in a Mentimeter ranking poll. See how the room orders them. Discuss the biggest disagreements.

Story of a Value

Think of a moment when you saw a youth worker live out a value really well — with young people, not in a meeting. Tell your neighbour in 60 seconds. Swap. No-one presents back.

Which Values Win Under Pressure?🛠 Mentimeter

Vote: which value do you drop first when time is tight? When you're tired? When there's conflict? Show results. Be honest — the honest vote is the useful one.

Facilitator notes
  • Use three separate Menti polls (single-choice) with the same value list
  • Don't comment on individual answers — let the pattern speak
Values Drift

On a sticky note (private — no-one reads it aloud), write one value your organisation claims but you don't see in practice. Fold it. Add it to a pile on a table. Acknowledge the pile exists, then move on.

ACTIVITY 15 – 75 min 6 activities — pick what fits
Pick Your Value

Choose ONE value from the word cloud. Write it in your phone's notes app. Don't share it with anyone — this is the value you're going to deliberately embed in a digital activity.

Facilitator notes
  • Encourage people to pick a value that feels slightly uncomfortable or contested — not the easy one
Build It🛠 Padlet or Mentimeter

In pairs, design a 10-minute digital activity that embeds your value. Must have clear instructions, be something a young person could actually do, and embed the value in a way that should be visible from the outside.

Facilitator notes
  • Have 4–5 blank Padlets / Menti templates ready so pairs aren't stuck in setup
  • Stop them at 20 mins regardless of readiness — rough is fine
Run It / Observe🛠 Padlet or Mentimeter

Swap with another pair. They run YOUR activity without your help. You watch silently. Note: where does the value show up? What does the design tell you about what they believe?

Predict the Value🛠 Mentimeter

Before the reveal, observers submit their guess in Mentimeter (open text): 'What value do you think this pair was trying to embed?' Submit first, then hear the answer.

The Reveal

Each pair reads their value out loud. Compare with observer guesses. Did it land? Where was the gap? Be honest — the gap is where the learning is.

The Value Gap

On the whiteboard, two columns: 'What I tried to embed' and 'What observers saw'. Everyone adds a sticky note to each column for their own activity. Walk the wall as a group.

KEY IDEAS 75 – 95 min 4 activities — pick what fits

The Gap Between What We Say and What We Do

  • Stated values vs observable behaviour — they're often different, and that's normal
  • Under pressure (disengagement, tech failure, time running out), your real priorities come out
  • System constraints force trade-offs: you can't have full freedom AND full safety at the same time
  • The question isn't 'what do I believe?' — it's 'what did I actually build?'
Pressure Scenarios🛠 Mentimeter

4 scenarios appear on screen one at a time. For each, vote live: which value would you sacrifice first? Show results. Where the room splits, that's the discussion.

Examples

  • Half the group isn't engaging. You have 5 minutes left. Do you push on or change the plan?
  • A young person says something offensive in the chat. Do you delete it, address it publicly, or message them privately?
  • The Wi-Fi goes down mid-session. You have 20 young people looking at you. What do you do?
  • Two participants are dominating. Others have gone silent. Do you intervene or let it play out?
Value Trade-Off Poll🛠 Mentimeter

Four value pairs appear one at a time: Safety vs Freedom · Inclusion vs Efficiency · Fun vs Learning · Control vs Agency. For each, vote: which side do you prioritise? No middle option. Discuss the split.

Stated vs Shown

In pairs, look at your activity from earlier. Write on a sticky note: 'This activity states [value] but actually shows [value].' Not every activity matches its intent — that's the exercise.

The Brand vs The Behaviour

In groups of 3, name 3 well-known organisations (public examples, not your own) whose stated values contradict their practice. What's the gap? Why does it exist?

DISCUSSION 95 – 150 min 5 activities — pick what fits
Redesign Under Constraint🛠 Padlet

Go back to your activity. Add one constraint: half the group have no smartphone / one participant has anxiety about being visible / you only have 5 minutes. Redesign in Padlet. What did you drop? What value did you compromise?

The Tool Dilemma

In pairs: name a platform you use with young people that you know compromises your values (Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, whatever). Why do you still use it? What's the trade-off you've accepted? Share back in one sentence.

Fishbowl Discussion

4 chairs in the middle of the room. Volunteers sit and discuss. Anyone can tap a shoulder to swap in. Everyone else listens in silence. Use the primers below to get going — pick whichever one hits hardest, or move through them as the conversation opens up.

Examples

  • When is it OK to compromise your values in youth work — and when isn't it?
  • Name a time you stayed silent when you should have spoken up. What stopped you?
  • Have you ever picked the tool that was easy over the tool that was right? What happened?
  • If a young person asked you 'do you actually believe this?' about something you're running — could you answer honestly?
  • What's a value your org says loudly that you've quietly stopped believing in?
  • When your values clash with your manager's, whose win — and why?
Facilitator notes
  • Project the primers on screen so the fishbowl volunteers can glance up at them
  • Start with primer 1 or 2. Don't read all of them — drop a new one in only when the conversation stalls
  • Keep to 12 minutes — stop when the conversation naturally slows
  • If no-one swaps, the original 4 stay. That's fine.
Principles Not Platforms🛠 Padlet

In groups of 3, write 5 principles that would guide your practice no matter what tool you use. Post each on a shared Padlet. Walk the Padlet as a group. Where do the principles overlap? Where do they disagree?

Hot Seat

One volunteer claims a value out loud. Others ask questions for 3 minutes, challenging whether that value actually shows up in their practice. No gotchas — just genuine 'how does that actually work when…' questions.

Facilitator notes
  • Only run this if the room is warm enough — otherwise skip
  • Time-box it strictly — 3 mins per volunteer
CLOSING 150 – 180 min 4 activities — pick what fits

"If the platform disappeared tomorrow, what principles would still guide you?"

Tools change. Apps shut down. But the way you think about young people's needs doesn't have to. The principle matters more than the platform.

Principle Statement🛠 Mentimeter

Complete the sentence in Menti: 'Regardless of what tool I use, I will always ___.' Submit live. Show the wall. Read a few aloud.

One Value I'm Watching

On a sticky note: one value you're going to actively check yourself on this week. Stick it on the wall. Photograph the wall — it's your commitment.

The Gap🛠 Mentimeter

Submit three things in Menti: one value I say I hold · one value I actually embody · one value I want to grow into. Read a few aloud.

Exit Ticket

Sticky on the door frame as you leave: one word for how you feel about the value you chose today. Read them back at the start of Session 3.

Your Takeaway
Review one activity you currently run. What values does it actually embed? What values are missing?
