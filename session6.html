Session 6 of 6

Build Something Real

Practitioners build, test, and improve a functional digital youth work activity for a real group.

📊 Download Slides (.pptx) 📄 Facilitator Guide (.docx)

Learning Objectives (LIA Template)

Aim

Practitioners build, test, and improve a functional digital youth work activity for a real group.

Objectives

  • Build a working digital activity for a specific youth group in 45 minutes
  • Submit to peer testing and receive structured feedback
  • Iterate based on evidence, not opinion
  • Evaluate against four quality criteria: participation, safety, agency, adaptability

Outcomes

  • Know how to: Take a digital youth work idea from concept to working prototype
  • Understand: The first version is never right — testing and iteration is the skill
  • Equipped with: Quality criteria for evaluating digital youth work
  • Action: Deploy the activity with a real group within 4 weeks

Session Plan

Structured across five phases (D-A-K-D-C). All activities include tool, timing, and instructions.

DISCOVER0 – 15 min

"What does good digital youth work actually look like?"

You've spent five sessions learning about digital spaces. Now describe what good looks like — not in theory, but from what you've seen and done.

Best Moment5 min

Everyone writes one sentence: the best digital interaction you've had with a young person (or observed). Read them out. What made it work?

Quality Criteria Vote8 min🛠 Mentimeter ranking

Four criteria on screen: Participation, Safety, Agency, Adaptability. Rank them in order of importance for YOUR group. Show results. Discuss disagreements.

ACTIVITY15 – 75 min
Define Your Group5 min

Write on a card: Who is this for? (Age, context, needs). What do they actually need? What tool will you use? Keep it specific — 'young people' is not a group.

Build It (45 min timer — strict)45 min🛠 Any tool from the course (Kahoot, Padlet, Mentimeter, Google Forms, WhatsApp, Discord)

Build a working activity. Not a plan. Not a presentation. Something another person could actually run right now. Rules: must be functional, must be for your specific group, must be completable. If you're still planning at the 10-minute mark, stop planning and start building.

Self-Check10 min

Before peer testing, rate your own activity against the 4 criteria (Participation, Safety, Agency, Adaptability). Score 1-5 each. Write it on a card. Keep it — you'll compare later.

KEY IDEAS75 – 95 min

What Good Looks Like

  • Participation — Are young people genuinely engaged or just compliant? Would they choose to do this?
  • Safety — Built into the design, not just a policy. Works when no facilitator is watching.
  • Agency — Real choices. Young people can shape the experience, not just consume it.
  • Adaptability — Works when conditions change. Different group size, different energy, different tech.
  • Common failures: Over-designed (too complex), Under-tested (works in your head only), Built for adults (solves your problem, not theirs).
Failure Mode Bingo10 min

Facilitator describes 6 common failure modes. Participants put a hand up if they think their build might have that problem. It's OK — the point is to catch it now, not after deployment.

Examples

  • Too many steps for the time available
  • Instructions only make sense to you
  • No way for young people to give feedback or make choices
  • Only works on one device or platform
  • Solves a problem the young people don't actually have
  • Would fall apart if 3 extra people joined
DISCUSSION95 – 150 min
Peer Test — Round 120 min

Swap with another pair. They run YOUR activity without your help. You watch silently. They give structured feedback: score each of the 4 criteria 1-5, plus one thing that worked and one thing that didn't.

Iterate10 min

Based on the feedback, fix ONE thing. Write on your iteration log: 'I changed ___ because ___. The feedback said ___.'

Peer Test — Round 215 min

Different pair tests your revised activity. Same feedback process. Write the second iteration on your log.

Before vs After10 min

Compare your self-check scores from earlier with the peer feedback scores. Where were you right? Where were you wrong? What assumption did you have to correct?

CLOSING150 – 180 min

"If the tool changed tomorrow, what standards would still apply?"

Tools will keep changing. The standards you've developed — participation, safety, agency, adaptability — those don't expire.

Lightning Presentations15 min

Each pair: 2 minutes. What you built. What broke. What you fixed. One assumption you had to correct.

Programme Reflection10 min🛠 Mentimeter or paper

Four questions: Most valuable session and why. Biggest shift in your thinking. What you'll do differently from Monday. What should change about this programme for the next group.

Final Takeaway5 min

Write one sentence that captures what you've learnt across all 6 sessions. Read it to the group.

Your Takeaway
Deploy your activity with a real group within 4 weeks. Document what happens. Submit a 1-2 page written justification within 2 weeks.
