Session 1 of 6

What Is Digital Youth Work?

Practitioners experience digital youth work from a young person's perspective and identify how environment design is practice.

📊 Download Slides (.pptx) 📄 Facilitator Guide (.docx)

Learning Objectives (LIA Template)

Aim

Practitioners experience digital youth work from a young person's perspective and identify how environment design is practice.

Objectives

  • Experience a digital activity as a learner, not a facilitator
  • Identify how changing one setting changes the whole experience
  • Distinguish between digitised youth work and digital youth work
  • Name one design choice that expresses a youth work value

Outcomes

  • Know how to: Set up and run a simple digital activity (Kahoot/Mentimeter)
  • Understand: Environment design IS practice — every toggle is a youth work decision
  • Equipped with: The question 'What am I designing when I set up this space?'
  • Action: Run one digital activity with your group this week

Session Plan

Structured across five phases (D-A-K-D-C). Each phase has more activities than you can fit — pick what matches your group.

DISCOVER 0 – 15 min 5 activities — pick what fits

"How does youth work change when young people are online?"

This isn't about technology. It's about whether the things you already do as a youth worker — building trust, giving young people a voice, keeping them safe — work the same way on a screen.

Spectrum Line

Stand along an imaginary line: one end = 'I use digital tools every day with young people', other end = 'I barely use anything digital'. Look at where everyone is. Brief chat about why.

Quick-Fire Word Cloud🛠 Mentimeter

Everyone submits 3 words that come to mind when they hear 'digital youth work'. Show the word cloud live on screen. What stands out? What's missing?

Pocket Audit

Everyone opens their phone, names one app they use daily, and says why. What does this tell us about what makes platforms sticky — for us and for young people?

Yes / No / Maybe Corners

Facilitator reads 4 statements. Participants move to the Yes, No, or Maybe corner of the room. Keep it quick — don't debate, just move.

Examples

  • I'd be comfortable running a Zoom session with young people tomorrow
  • My organisation has a digital strategy I actually understand
  • I know which platform my young people use most
  • Digital youth work is something we do properly, not just a backup
The Time Machine

Close your eyes. Picture a 14-year-old from 10 years ago — what was their digital life? Now picture a 14-year-old today. What's changed? Share one thing with a neighbour.

ACTIVITY 15 – 75 min 8 activities — pick what fits
Play the Quiz (as a young person)🛠 Kahoot

Join a pre-made Kahoot quiz on your phones. No briefing — just play. Experience the timer, the leaderboard, the music, the pressure. Don't overthink it.

  • Facilitator runs a 10-question Kahoot (topic: general knowledge or fun)
  • Participants play on their phones as if they were young people
  • No talking — just play the game as it comes
Observer Round

Half the group plays a shorter quiz (5 questions) while the other half silently watches. Observers use a worksheet: Who looks stressed? Who lights up? Who disengages? Who's first to answer? Who checks their phone between questions?

What Just Happened?

Debrief the experience before changing anything.

  • Show of hands: Who felt competitive? Who felt stressed? Who stopped caring?
  • On sticky notes: one thing that felt good, one thing that felt bad
  • Stick them on the board in two columns. Read a few out.
  • Observers share what they saw — not what players said, what they saw
Change One Thing🛠 Kahoot

In pairs, pick ONE setting to change and re-run part of the quiz.

  • Options: remove the timer / hide the leaderboard / enable anonymous answers / team mode / remove the music
  • Facilitator sets up a shorter quiz with the change
  • Re-run. What's different? Is it better or worse? For who?
The Anonymous Round🛠 Kahoot

Re-run with anonymous nicknames (no real names on the leaderboard). Does anything shift? Who contributes now who didn't before? Who goes quieter? Note it down.

Settings Gallery

Pin a card on the wall for each Kahoot setting (timer, leaderboard, music, team mode, anonymous, chat, skip-allowed). In pairs, walk round and add a sticky note to each: 'What this changes for the young person.' Read the wall as a group.

Design Decision Cards

Each pair fills in a card: 'We changed ___ because ___. The effect was ___. For a young person who ___ this would mean ___.' Share back to the group.

Second Pass — Swap🛠 Kahoot

Observers now play; players now observe. Different setting changed. Observers: what do you notice now that you missed when you were playing? Players: what's different knowing someone is watching?

KEY IDEAS 75 – 95 min 4 activities — pick what fits

Digital vs Digitised Youth Work

  • Digitised = taking what you already do and putting it on a screen (paper form → Google Form)
  • Digital = designing an experience that only works because it's online (anonymous polling, real-time collaboration, adaptive difficulty)
  • The environment IS the practice — every setting (timer, names, chat, scores) is a youth work decision you're making for the young person
Sort the Examples

Facilitator reads out 8 examples. Participants hold up a card: 'DIGITISED' or 'DIGITAL'. Discuss any disagreements.

Examples

  • A paper register replaced by a Google Form → DIGITISED
  • A Kahoot quiz with anonymous mode so shy young people contribute → DIGITAL
  • Emailing a worksheet instead of printing it → DIGITISED
  • A Discord server where young people moderate their own channels → DIGITAL
  • Filming a session and putting it on YouTube → DIGITISED
  • A Padlet board where young people build a campaign together in real time → DIGITAL
  • Scanning a paper evaluation into a PDF → DIGITISED
  • An Instagram poll asking young people to vote on next week's activity → DIGITAL
Sort Your Own Practice

Two boards on the wall: DIGITISED and DIGITAL. Everyone takes one activity they currently run and sticks it on whichever board it fits. Be honest — if it's digitised paper, that's fine, just name it. Walk the wall.

Values Hidden in Settings

Facilitator shows a setting (leaderboard, timer, chat disabled, anonymous mode). In pairs, write down what value that setting expresses. Compare answers across the room. Same setting, different values? That's the conversation.

One Slide, One Question

Facilitator projects a screenshot of a Kahoot setup screen. Each pair writes one question they'd ask the person who set it up about why they made each choice. Read a few aloud — you've just written your own facilitator audit.

DISCUSSION 95 – 150 min 6 activities — pick what fits
Tool Speed-Date🛠 Mentimeter + Padlet + Kahoot

3-minute rotations. Each table has a different tool open. Try it for 3 minutes, then rotate. At the end, vote: which felt most useful for YOUR group?

Design Your Own🛠 Paper or Padlet

In pairs, sketch a 10-minute digital activity you could run with your group next week. Use any tool. Keep it simple. Write: What tool? What do participants do? What's the one setting you'd change to make it work for YOUR group?

Pitch Your Activity

Each pair gets 90 seconds to pitch their activity to another pair. No slides, no notes — just speak. The listening pair asks ONE question. Swap.

Objection Panel

One pair presents their design to the room. Others play devil's advocate: 'What if the Wi-Fi dies?' 'What about the quietest young person?' 'What if someone doesn't have a phone?' The presenting pair doesn't need to solve every problem — just acknowledge it.

Mix and Match

Pairs swap their design with another pair. Add one constraint: no Wi-Fi / one participant is non-verbal / half the group have no phone / there are 25 people not 10. Redesign in 10 minutes.

Gallery Walk

Pairs pin their (now-redesigned) activity on the wall or post to a shared Padlet. Everyone walks around, reads them, and leaves one sticky note of feedback on each: 'Have you thought about…'

CLOSING 150 – 180 min 4 activities — pick what fits

"What practice decision did you just make — without saying a word?"

Every time you set up a digital space, you're making decisions about who can speak, who can see what, and who has control. Those are youth work decisions, not tech decisions.

One Thing I'll Try🛠 Mentimeter or sticky notes

Everyone submits one thing they'll try with their group before the next session. Show results on screen. Photograph the wall.

Commitment Partner

Partner with someone NOT from your organisation. Swap contact details. Agree one thing you'll each try and check in in 2 weeks. Write it on a card and keep it.

The Gap

On a paper: 'One thing I knew before this session. One thing I know now. One thing I still don't know.' Fold it. Hand it to the facilitator on the way out.

Exit Ticket

Sticky note on the door frame as you leave: one word that captures how you feel about running a digital activity this week. Read them back at the start of Session 2.

Your Takeaway
Run one digital activity with your group this week. It doesn't have to be perfect.
